Study in the UK

The UK has been ranked globally and is known for its undisputed reputation in the field of education. It has numerous universities and colleges at par with the best in the world. The course curriculum is one of the most advanced with an added advantage of lesser duration. 

The UK attracts millions of international students from over 86 countries, offering them opportunities for the overall development. Having educated millions of students, the UK is the most preferred education destination for students from across the globe.

Why Study in the UK?

The ease of securing a student visa to study in the UK, makes the post application process easier than expected. Along with this there are other factors which are responsible for influencing students to study in the UK, like:

Internationally Recognised Universities

Universities in the UK have a remarkable international reputation.

According to QS World University Rankings, 4 of the top 6 universities of the world are in the UK.

Great Place to Live and Study

UK is a very tolerant society. It has a great cultural diversity. Discrimination against people is illegal and a punishable offence in the UK.  

Shorter Duration of Courses

Short duration courses in the UK help reduce tuition and accommodation costs.

Strong Research Infrastructure

  • The latest Research Excellence Framework (REF) classifies 30% of the UK university research as 'world-leading'.
  • 46% is considered as 'internationally excellent'

Scholarship and Financial Aid

The UK offers up to 100% Scholarship on tuition fees in selected Universities only.

Work Opportunities

If visa permits, the international students can work while studying in the UK. Which is usually 20 hours per week.

Top Scholarships for international students in the UK

Scholarships for post graduate and doctoral courses in the UK are not open for all the candidates, as they have strict eligibility criteria.

1. Chevenching Scholarships

  • Level of study: Masterâs;
  • Countries eligible: 144 countries. Around 700 scholarships are awarded every year.
  • Applications dates: August - October/November (usually).

2. Commonwealth Scholarships

  • Level of study: Masterâs and PhD (undergraduate-citizens of St. Helena and the Seychelles only)
  • Countries eligible: 62 commonwealth nations
  • Eligibility criteria: commonwealth citizen, refugee or British protected person and at least 2:1 Honours degree.
  • Applications open: August before the course is due to start
  • Amount: Typically covers tuition fees, a monthly living allowance and travel to and from the UK.

3. Saltire Scholarships

  • Level of study: Master's

  • Other eligibility criteria: Offers a place at Scottish university to study Master's in any one-Science, Technology, Creative Industries, Healthcare and Medical Sciences or Renewable and Clean Energy 
  • Applications open: October, till May.

4. The Australia Foundation Trust

  • Eligibility Criteria: Australian citizens entitled to live and study in the UK
  • Level of study: All levels
  • Applications date: November - April

5. Mackenzie King Memorial Scholarships

  • Level of study: Master's/PhD
  • Eligibility criteria: Degree from any Canadian university with a minimum grade A, across all courses
