JEE Advanced 2020 Exam Pattern: Marking Scheme & Types of Questions

JEE Advanced 2020 is a computer-based exam, it consists of 2 papers- Paper 1 and Paper 2 of 3 hours duration each. Both these papers are compulsory and IITs haven’t fixed a pattern for these papers. The conducting IIT always surprises students taking the exam. Read JEE Advanced Syllabus

Latest: JEE Advanced 2020 is now scheduled for September 27, 2020. The notifications regarding the dates of the rest of the exam-related events are expected soon. 

JEE Advanced has MCQs, numeric value-based answer questions, etc. An exclusive feature of JEE Advanced is that the marking scheme for JEE Advanced comprises full, zero, and partial marks. Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) is held separately after JEE Advanced.

Based on previous years’ statistics/trends, we have enlisted the marking scheme and exam pattern for JEE Advanced 2020. Read on to know more about JEE Advanced Paper Pattern without sullying the time.

JEE Advanced 2020 Marking Scheme

The number of questions in JEE Advanced is not fixed. They vary every year. So, maximum marks cannot be defined. From the previous years’ analysis, here we have inferred the marking scheme of JEE Advanced Pattern 2020. Candidates can check the expected marking scheme from the table below:

Section Question type Marks awarded Negative Marking
1 Single correct answer 3 -1
2 1 or more correct answer 4 -1
3 Numerical Value Answer Based Question 3 0

Paper-2 also consists of 10 sets of questions and the marking scheme followed is as follows:

Section Question type Marks awarded Negative Marking
1 1 or more correct options 4 -1
2 Numerical Value-Based Answer 3 0
3 List Match Sets 3 -1

For both paper 1 and paper 2 questions will be asked from class 11 and 12 syllabi. Candidates who are interested in pursuing B.Arch have to appear for AAT (Architecture Aptitude Test).

JEE Advanced AAT Pattern

Just like the paper pattern of JEE Advanced, the AAT pattern is not fixed either. Check the brief exam pattern of AAT:

  • Mode of Examination: Offline
  • Duration of Exam: 3 hours
  • Language Medium: English
  • Sections: Freehand Drawing, Geometrical Drawing, Three Dimensional Perception, Imagination, and Aesthetic Sensitivity, Architectural Awareness.

JEE Advanced 2020 Exam Day Guidelines

  • Candidates must reach exam centers 20 minutes before.
  • Kindly read the exam day instructions written on the admit card and don’t carry any of the prohibited items to the exam hall.
  • Carry the admit card and id proof along with you.
  • Do not leave the exam center without submitting the ORS sheet and calculation sheet
  • Candidates must coordinate during the ID verification and biometric verification.
  • The computers will be assigned to you, your name shall be displayed on it. Check the details and login carefully when instructed to.
  • Rough should be done only on the pad provided. No extra scribble bad will be provided to the candidate, so use it properly.
  • No extra time shall be given to complete the exam, so candidates must keep a check on time and complete their exams.
