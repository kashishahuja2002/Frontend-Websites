JEE Advanced Cutoff 2020, Qualifying Marks and Previous Years' Cutoff

IIT Delhi released JEE Advanced 2020 qualifying cut off through the information brochure. However, the revised and the final cut off will be released after the declaration of JEE Advanced Result. Candidates clearing the qualifying cut off will be included in the common rank list.

Candidates have to qualify JEE Advanced cutoff 2020 individually for all three subjects and also aggregate for all three subjects to qualify the exam. JEE Advanced cutoff 2020 will be different for various categories. The candidates who will qualify JEE Advanced shall be eligible for admission in IITs. Along with cut off, merit list of qualified candidates is also released. JEE Advanced Cut off will be released in the form of Opening and Closing Ranks for every participating institute.  

JEE Advanced Result will be released on June 8, 2020. The official cut off for this session will be released shortly after the result declaration. Students can read the article below to know more about JEE Advanced Cut Off 2020.

Highlights of JEE Advanced Cut Off 2020

  • Students qualifying JEE Advanced cutoff will be considered eligible for admission in IITs.
  • JEE Advanced Cut off list will be different for different categories. 
  • Candidates must secure a rank above the cut off to secure a seat.
  • The seat allotment will be solely based on category wise All India Ranks (AIR) in JEE Advanced 2020.
  • Rank lists will be prepared for each of the participating institutes individually. The minimum prescribed marks are also declared category-wise. Read About JEE Advanced Counselling
  • Students will be provided the information about JEE Advanced 2020 cutoff as soon as it will be released by IIT Delhi.
  • Cut off list will contain the Minimum Percentage of Marks in Each Subject and Minimum Percentage of Aggregate Marks.

Factors Affecting Cut Off

Factors Affecting JEE Advanced Cutoff

The cut off of JEE Advanced is set after considering the following factors:

  • Number of students participated in the exam
  • The complexity level of the exam
  • Seat Matrix of colleges 
  • Previous years’ cutoff trend 
  • Candidates’ performance in JEE Advanced
