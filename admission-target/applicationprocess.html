JEE Advanced 2020 Application Form (Released): Register at jeeadv.ac.in

IIT Delhi has released JEE Advanced 2020 Application Forms on September 12, 2020, for JEE Main qualified students. Candidates can submit their application forms through online mode at jeeadv.ac.in till September 17, 2020. Final date for fee payment is September 18, 2020 (Friday, 5:00 Pm). Also, JEE Advanced Registration for foreign candidates has been opened on September 5, 2020.

  • JEE Advanced Eligibility: Applicants must have a rank within the top 2,50,000 in JEE Main 2020 across all categories fulfill the minimum eligibility criteria of JEE Advanced 2020
  • JEE Advanced Registration Fees (Increased): There is an increase in Application fees for candidates of different categories. General and OBC (male and transgender) candidates have to pay INR 2800. The Application fees for females and other reserved categories is INR 1400.
  • JEE Advanced Admit Card: JEE Advanced Admit Card will be issued on September 21, 2020. The document can be downloaded from the official website using JEE Advanced registration number, date of birth (DD-MM-YYYY), mobile number and email address.
  • JEE Advanced Reservation: There will be reservation for General EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST and PwD category candidates. A valid proof of eligibility must be submitted in order to avail JEE Advanced Reservation

How to apply for JEE Advanced?

How to Fill JEE Advanced Application Form?

JEE Advanced registration for Indian students has been released on September 12, 2020. Foreign candidates are not required to write JEE Main and may register for JEE Advanced 2020 directly. Partly filled application forms are rejected summarily by the authorities.

Check the following steps to apply for JEE Advanced 2020:

Step 1: JEE Advanced 2020 Registration

  • Visit the official website of JEE Advanced 2020.
  • Enter JEE Main 2020 Roll Number and Password.
  • Select a new password.
  • You will be redirected to a new page containing personal and academic details.
  • Provide basic data such as the language of question paper (English or Hindi), cities for the examination, etc.
  • Provide 6 preferred cities/towns where you would want to take the exam. 
  • Save and move to the next step.

Step 2: Upload Documents

Documents required must be kept handy prior to filling the application form. Once the JEE Advanced application form is filled correctly, upload the required set of documents with the specifications.

  • Upload high quality scanned images of your latest photograph and signature in JPEG format.
  • The photograph should be more than 4kb and less than 40 kb in size
  • The signature size should range from 1kb- 30kb.
  • All other documents size should be more than 50kn and less than 300kb.

Step 3: Payment of Application Fee

JEE Advanced Registration Fee is neither refundable nor transferable. Pay the application fee and take the print out of duly filled application at the end for further use. Note that JEE Advanced 2020 Application Form cannot be withdrawn after completion of registration.

Step 4: Printing of Confirmation Page

After the payment of fees, aspirants can submit the application form and save the confirmation page containing all the details for future references.

Essential Documents

Documents Required for JEE Advanced Registration 2020

Given below is a list of certificates and other documents that are to be uploaded at the time of filling JEE Advanced Application Form:

Documents Required For All Candidates

  • In case the name is not same as in the Class 10 certificate, gazette notification is required showing the change of name.
  • Class 10 certificate if the Date of Birth is mentioned in it OR birth certificate.
  • Class 12 (or equivalent examination) certificate (for those who appeared for this examination in 2020 or whose board declared the Class 12 (or equivalent examination) result after June 2016).

Additional Documents (if applicable)

  • OBC-NCL Certificate should have been issued on or after April 01, 2020 as per the latest guidelines and updates on www.ncbc.in. Click Here for the format
  • Caste Certificate for SC/ST category, as per the latest guidelines of the Government of India. Click Here for the format
  • Certificate for PwD category: Physical Disability certificate (FORM-PwD II/III/IV whichever is applicable]) issued by the notified medical authority. Click Here for the format
    • Dyslexic candidate needs to submit FORM-DYSLEXIC 1 and 2 instead of FORM-PwD. The certificate must mention ‘SEVERE’ to enable the candidate get the benefit of PwD category.
    • Individual IITs may constitute a medical board at the time of admission to test the candidate’s level of disability.
  • For candidates needing the services of a scribe: Request letter to the Chairman of IIT Roorkee (Form – Scribe).
  • For candidates seeking admission under DS category: Certificate (FORM-DS) issued by a competent authority in the Directorate of Resettlement and Rehabilitation, New Delhi under the Ministry of Defence
    or the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India. Click Here for the format
  • For PIO/OCI Card Holders: PIO/OCI Certificate, as applicable.

Documents Required For Foreign Candidates

  • Birth certificate for age proof
  • Class XII marks sheet/certificate for 75% cut-off (if available at the time of registration)
  • Identity proof of the candidate
  • Citizenship certificate/Passport of both the parents
  • Testimonial (if required)
Registration Fee

JEE Advanced Registration Fee (Revised)

Candidates can pay registration fee through online (debit card & net banking) or offline (SBI Challan) mode. The fee is neither refundable nor transferable.

The fee details are given below:

Registration Fee for Examination Centers in India
Indian Nationals (including PIO/OCI) Female Candidates (all categories) INR 1400
SC, ST, PwD Candidates INR 1400
All Other Candidates INR 2800
Foreign Nationals Candidates from SAARC countries USD 75
Candidates from Non-SAARC countries USD 150
Registration Fee for Examination Centers outside India
Indian Nationals (including PIO/OCI) All India Nationals USD 75
Foreign Nationals Candidates from SAARC countries USD 75
Candidates from Non-SAARC countries USD 150

Payment by SBI Debit Card/Internet Banking

At the time of payment of Application Fee, option of “Pay Registration Fee” will be displayed. Click on it and system will automatically redirect to State Bank Multi Option Payment System (MOPS) page.

As per the candidate’s convenience, they may select any of the available options such as SBI Debit Card or Internet Banking. After successful payment, candidate will be able to print the completed Registration Details page with UNIQUE Registration Number.

Payment by Cash using SBI Branch Option

In case of Cash payment, select “SBI Branch” and system will automatically generate bank challan. Take a printout of it and pay the application fee in cash at any SBI branch.

After successful payment, candidate will be able to print complete registration details page with Unique Registration Number.

Payment for Exam Centres Outside India

Candidates seeking Exam centres outside India are required to pay the registration fee as mentioned above through International Debit or Credit Card issued by Foreign Banks and issued outside India. Payment in US $ (dollar) is not allowed through Debit or Credit Cards issued in India. After successful payment, candidate will be able to print the completed Registration Details page with Unique Registration Number.

Retrieving Application Number and Password

In case candidates forget their application number and password, there is an option of retrieval by performing the steps given below. Steps to reset the password and retrieve application number are:

  1. Applicants can use the security question and its answer chosen during filling JEE Advanced 2020 registration form.
  2. The verification code sent via text message to registered mobile number of candidate
  3. A reset link sent via Email to the registered Email address of candidate

Mistakes to avoid during JEE Advanced Registration

  • Candidates must ensure that he/she has paid JEE Advanced application fee successfully before the last date. If the transaction remains incomplete, the application form will not be acceptable.
  • Do not upload the required documents in any other format other than format or size specified above.
  • Do not opt for test canters that are distant. Choose your JEE Advanced test centres that are nearby to your residence.

Documents to be kept after JEE Advanced Registration

After the completion of the application process of JEE Advanced , candidates are requested to keep the following documents safe:

  • JEE Advanced registration confirmation mail
  • Passport size photographs,similar to the one used while filling JEE Advanced application form.
  • A printed JEE Advanced 2020 Admit Card
  • In case of any technical error, candidates must have a proof of registration.

International Applicants Guidelines

Important information for foreign candidates who wish to apply for JEE Advanced are:

  • Candidates who are not citizens of India (by birth or naturalized), do not belong to OCI category, and whose parents are not citizens of India and do not come under OCI category at the time of registering for JEE Advanced 2020 are treated as foreign nationals.
  • Supernumerary seats are allotted to foreign nationals with a cap of 10% of the total number of seats in every course.
  • Foreign nationals are not eligible for the reservation of seats under the OBC‐NCL, SC, ST and PwD categories as mandated by Govt. of India.
  • These candidates are not required to write JEE(MAIN) and may register for JEE(Adv) 2020 directly subject to fulfilment of other eligibility criteria.
  • Identity Proof: Identity proof of both the parents is required in the form of citizenship certificate/Passport
