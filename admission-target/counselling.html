JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling by JoSAA Dates (Released), Seat Allotment and Counselling Process

JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling & seat allotment will start from in October 6, 2020 and will be conducted by JoSAA. Candidates who have qualified JEE Main and JEE Advanced are eligible to participate in JoSAA Counselling. JoSAA conducts three main processes of Registering, Filling and Locking Choices. The seats are allotted to students based on their All India Rank in the qualifying exam. Check JEE Advanced Cutoff 

A total of 107 institutes participate in JoSAA Counselling. The list of courses along with the seat matrix is available on the official website of JoSAA i.e. josaa.nic.in. The counseling will be conducted in 7 rounds which may vary depending on the availability of seats. 

There are 23 IITs which offer 11,279 seats in total. Candidates applying for JoSAA Counselling for admission to IITs have to report at the allotted center for document verification. There are 18 reporting centers for IITs whereas 45 reporting centers are available for NITs, IIITs, and Other GFTIs. Eligible candidates can withdraw their seats in any of the counseling rounds except the last round. The introduction of seat withdrawal option by the JoSAA officials will be significant in the reduction of the number of unfilled seats at IITs, NITs, IIITs and GFTIs. Check JEE Advanced Result

IITs Demand More Time to fill EWS Quota

IITs Ask for Another Year to fill EWS Quota

  • IITs collectively need to increase their student intake for undergraduate courses, masters and research programmes by about 6700 seats for accommodating the 10 per cent Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) quota.
  • Out of the target of adding 6700 seats, approximately 2,300 seats (about 500 for B.Tech ) were added in 2019-20. The remaining 4,400 seats (about 1,300 for Btech) were expected to be added this year.
  • IITs have sought a year’s extension to the 2021 deadline due the challenges of maintaining social distancing in the hostels once the institutions reopen.
  • 23 IITs have collectively approached the Centre with the request recently. 
  • To ensure that existing number of seats for SC, ST, OBC and general category students does not face cuts, all centrally funded educational institutions, including IITs have to increase their overall student strength by 25 percent, over two years, to accommodate the quota.
Result and Rank List

JEE Advanced Result 2020

  • The result for JEE Advanced will be declared expectedly in the first week of October at the official website of JEE Advanced.
  • Candidates can check their result by entering their JEE Advanced 2020 Registration Number, Date of Birth and security key.
  • JEE Advanced Result will contain the All India Rank (AIR) and Category Rank of successful candidates.
  • Candidates who have secured minimum prescribed marks in each subject and in aggregate to appear in the JEE Advanced 2020 Merit list. 

JEE Advanced Tie Breaker Policy

If there is a clash between two or more candidates in terms of their aggregate marks, then the following tie resolving policy shall be used:

  • Aspirants with higher positive ranks will be given a higher rank.
  • Aspirants with greater marks in mathematics will be given a higher rank.
  • Aspirants with greater marks in Physics will be given a higher rank.
  • If the tie situation still continues, then it will be resolved by assigning the same ranks to the candidates. 

JEE Advanced Rank List 2020

The candidates who appear for both Paper 1 and Paper 2 will be considered for ranking. Rank list is prepared on the basis of aggregate marks scored in JEE Advanced 2020, which is the sum of marks secured by a candidate in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics. To be included in the merit list, a candidate must clear the qualifying cut off. Eligibility for Counselling

Eligibility for JEE Advanced Counselling

Candidates who qualify JEE Advanced will be allowed to participate in JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling for admission to various UG courses offered by IITs. However, these candidates should fulfill the following eligibility criteria to take part in the counseling and seat allotment process.

  1. Candidates should have obtained a rank in Common Rank List in JEE Advanced.
  2. Earlier as per the criteria, the aggregate in the qualifying examination should not be less than 75% (65% for SC/ST/PwD) for the candidates. In the case of percentile, the candidate should be among the top 20 percentile their respective board examination.IITs have dropped the percentage criteria for admissions this year. IITs will grant admissions based on the performance of the candidate in JEE Advanced 2020 irrespective of their score in class 12th board exams (the candidate must have a passing certificate). 
  3. The aggregate marks obtained in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Language (Language paper in which candidates have scored higher marks will be taken into consideration) will be calculating the aggregate in qualifying examination. If any of these are not evaluated during the final year, (for eg. Three-year diploma courses) the marks of the previous year will be taken into consideration.
  4. Candidates who have failed in one or more than one subject in the qualifying exam should appear for the exam in 2016 and clear it in this attempt.
  5. In cases where the qualifying board offers only letter grades, the candidates are required to procure a letter from the board indicating the aggregate marks of the candidate. Otherwise, the decision in such matter will be taken by the Joint Implementation Committee of JEE Advanced 2020. The decision of the committee would be final and no request for any review would be considered.
  6. The marks of Class 12th would be considered only, in case, the board provides the marks for both Class 11th and 12th in the qualifying examination. If the board provides only the aggregate marks for qualifying examination, then the aggregate marks will be considered for evaluation.

Note: Candidates including foreign nationals will have to abide by the above-mentioned criteria

JEE Advanced Counselling Process

The first round of seat allocation will be held expectedly in October. The counselling process can be broadly divided into the following steps:

  • Registration for JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling
  • Choice Filling and Locking
  • Allotment of Seats
  • Reporting to Allotted Colleges
  • Granting Provisional Admission to candidates

Candidates should go through all the steps of JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling process, discussed below in detail.

JoSAA Counselling Registration 

Step 1: JoSAA Counselling Registration 

Candidates are first required to register online for JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling. Candidates will have to enter their JEE Main Roll Number and JEE Advanced Password to login and reconfirm the previously filled details. 

Filling-in and Locking of Choices

Step 2: Filling-in and Locking of Choices

Candidates will have to log in and fill up their choices as per branch, course and institute preference. They are permitted to change, edit, delete or add new choices until finalizing their choices. Candidates must lock their final choices before the due dates failing which leads to automated locking of choices. Applicants can select a maximum of 50 choices during the online locking of choices. They are advised to take a print out of their finally submitted choices.

Note:

  1. Fitness Criteria required to be fulfilled by students for Mining and Petroleum Engineering:

Candidates planning to apply for Mining and Mining Machinery Engineering and related courses must fulfill additional fitness criteria as mentioned below:

  • These students should be free from color blindness and the standard of visual acuity with or without glasses should be as per DGMS circular (the distant vision of the eye with or without glasses should be not less than 6/6 and 6/9 for a worse eye)
  • Candidates applying for Petroleum engineering and related courses as well as those seeking admission in M.Sc. / M. Tech. in Applied Geology must be free from clause of color blindness also.
  1. Special Eligibility for Female candidates in Mining Engineering Courses
  • ISM Dhanbad does not admit female students in Mining Engineering, Mining Machinery Engineering and any of the related courses.
  • Female candidates are, however, eligible for admission in Mining Engineering at IIT Kharagpur and IIT (BHU) Varanasi, provided they fulfill the special medical requirements.
  • The female candidates as per Section 46 (1) of the Mines Act, 1952, are not permitted to work in mines below ground. They are allowed to work only between 6 AM to 7 PM on mines above ground level.
  1. Admission to Architecture Courses in IITs
  • Candidates seeking admission in Architecture courses at the IITs should appear and pass the Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) conducted at the seven Zonal IITs. 
  • Candidates are required to choose B. Arch. at IIT Kharagpur or IIT Roorkee during online choice filling in counseling and appear for AAT 2020 also. JEE Advanced 2020 Admit Card issued would be required to appear for Architecture Aptitude Test.
  • Candidates would be however considered for admission, solely on the basis of JEE Advanced AIR and category rank of the candidate.
Online Seat Allotment

Step 3: JEE Advanced Online Seat Allotment

  • JoSAA will conduct two mock allotments to indicate the candidates about their probable allotment as per their current choices.
  • Candidates will be able to alter their choices after the mock allotment.
  • Allotment of seats will be done as per the merit, choices preferred category and seat availability.
  • JEE Advanced 2020 seat allotment would be conducted on the basis of All India Rank (AIR) secured by the candidates in JEE Advancedand the final choices filled by them after mock allotment. 
  • After each allotment, the candidates will have to option what they want to do with the allotted seat- float, freeze and slide.

How to check the status for seat allotment?

The candidates can follow the steps mentioned below to check the status of seat allotment during JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling. The opening and closing ranks for all the categories would vary. Hence, the candidates securing higher in their respective categories will be allotted seats accordingly.

  • Log in using the Registration No (User ID), JEE Advanced 2020 Roll No and Password to check their seat allotment status.
  • Students should check their status of Seat Allotment at the end of every round.
  • Allotments might change due to up-gradation. Hence, students should pay attention to this.
  • The soft copy of the category (SC/ST/OBC-NCL) certificates uploaded at the time of online registration will be scrutinized against originals during seat allocation
  • If the certificates uploaded are found to be inappropriate, the student will be treated as General only if their score in the Paper-1 of JEE Main is above the cutoff score for General category.

Note: The seat allocation in JEE Advanced 2020 Counselling would be completed within three rounds. All the candidates are therefore advised to check the status after the completion of every round, as there is a possibility that the seats allotted to them are upgraded because other students have either withdrawn from the course or have accepted seats they prefer.

Step 4: Reporting for JEE Advanced 2020 Seat Allotment

Candidates are required to report to the Zonal IIT after the seats are allotted to them. All the candidates should be present in person for verification of documents and payment of admission fee. The seat acceptance fee for General and reserved categories are mentioned below.

CATEGORY FEE (INR)
General/OBC 35000
SC/ST & PWD 15000

Some of the important points for reporting to allotted centers for taking provisional admission are mentioned below. Candidates who don’t report to the Zonal IIT before the stipulated deadline will not be considered for seat allotment and their seats will be automatically forfeited. In case, the applicants are offered an upgradation, they will have to forfeit the seat allotted to them earlier in order to accept the upgraded seat.

  • Candidates can pay the fee through any of the acceptable modes such as internet banking, credit card/debit card or payment in the nearest branch
  • They need to upload a declaration on acceptance form, after signing it themselves and by their parents. This form states that the student is eligible for admission as per the 'Top 20 Percentile Cut off Marks of their respective Board in their respective categories for the year of the passing of qualifying examination’.
  • All the applicants should also upload the scanned copy of marks sheet of 10 + 2.
  • Candidates allotted seats in any of the seven rounds should report to the respective institutes on the specified date and time.
Provisional Admission

Step 5: Provisional Admission

The institutes will issue a final allotment letter and grant provisional admission to candidates after verifying the documents and the fee is paid by the candidate. Candidates who fail to meet any eligibility criteria or are caught providing false information/documents will be debarred from the counselling process and their admission would be immediately deemed canceled.

Documents Required for Provisional Admission

Students are required to submit original documents along with one attested photocopy during verification for taking provisional admission.

  • JEE Advanced 2020 Original Admit Card
  • JEE Advanced 2020 Seat Allotment Letter from JOCSAP website
  • JEE Advanced Application Form (duly filled)
  • Duly filled medical examination report
  • Birth Proof — Class Xth /HSC/Birth Certificate/Any other certificate as proof of data of birth
  • Class 12th or Equivalent Examination Mark sheet and Passing Certificate
  • Category Certificate (OBC (NCL)/SC/ST/PwD/DS) issued by competent authority as per the format in the JEE Advanced 2020 Brochure
  • Criteria for Foreign National students — Attested photocopy of the page in the passport comprising the name, photo and nationality.
  • DD towards the registration fee. It would be later adjusted towards the tuition fee, to be paid at the time of admission.
