JEE Advanced 2021 Exam Date, Application Form, Pattern, Syllabus and Mock Test

Considered as one of the most prestigious entrance examinations in India, JEE Advanced is an engineering entrance examination conducted by any of the seven Indian Institute of Technology(s) viz. IIT Delhi, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Bombay, IIT Guwahati, IIT Madras, IIT Kanpur, IIT Roorkee or IISc Bangalore, under the support of Joint Admission Board (JAB) India. 

  • In order to appear for JEE Advanced 2021, students are first required to clear the JEE Main 2021 examination.
  • Only those students who are among the top 2,50,000 rank holders in JEE Main 2021 are eligible for appearing in JEE Advanced 2021. 
  • Qualified candidates will be offered admissions in B.tech, B.Arch., B.S programs etc. in various IITs and other prestigious engineering colleges across India. 
  • Online applications for the examination will be available in the month of May 2021 for the JEE advanced 2021. Read JEE Advanced 2021 Application Details.

The examination consists of two papers and three hours are allotted for each. The examination is conducted in the online mode in both English and Hindi. Both Paper 1 and Paper 2 are conducted on the same day. A candidate can appear only twice for JEE advanced examination.

JEE Advanced 2021 Eligibility

Candidates are required to fulfil the following eligibility criteria for filling the form for JEE Advanced:

  • Only those candidates who were born on or after 1st October 1997, are eligible to apply in JEE Advanced 2021. SC/ST/PWD category students are provided with a relaxation of 5 years.
  • Both Indian and foreign nationals are eligible for this examination.
  • Only those candidates who have appeared for the 12th examination for the first time in 2020 or are appearing in the year 2021 are eligible for this examination.
  • Candidates are required to qualify the JEE Main and be among the top-ranking students.
  • The maximum number of attempts for JEE Advanced is two.

JEE Advanced 2021 Application Process

  • The online application process for JEE Advanced will begin in the first week of May 2021.
  • The deadline for the online application submission will tentatively be the second week of May 2021.
  • Candidates are required to fill all the personal information including their email address and phone number(s). 
  • Candidates are also required to provide the medium of the paper.

Documents Required:

Candidates are required to submit the following documents at the time of registration:

  • Class 10th certificate for the purpose of validating the date of birth of the candidate.
  • Class 12th certificate.
  • Caste certificate wherever applicable.
  • Disability certificate wherever applicable.
  • PIO or OCI certificates wherever applicable.

JEE Advanced 2021 Admit Card

The admit card for the JEE Advanced 2021 will be out by May 2021. Steps to download your admit card - 

  • Candidates are required to log in to the official portal using their application number and password. Upon logging in, candidates will be able to download the admit card. 
  • The admit card will contain the details of the examination date and the venue for the examination. 
  • Candidates are required to take a print out of the admit card and carry the same during the examination. 
  • Candidates must note that there is no provision for delivering the hard copy of the admit card via post. 
  • Candidates are advised to retain the admit card till the end of the admission process.

JEE Advanced 2021 Exam Center

The examination centers for JEE advanced are divided into 7 zones- IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Kanpur, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Guwahati, IIT Hyderabad and IIT Roorkee Zone. Candidates are required to select their choice of the zone and examination city while filling up the application form.

JEE Advanced 2021 Exam Pattern

JEE Advanced 2021 will contain two papers - Paper 1 and Paper 2, with two language options viz. English and Hindi. 

  • JEE Advanced 2021 will be a computer-based test. 
  • Candidates will be given a time limit of 3 hours to attempt each paper.
  • The test will consist of Multiple Choice Questions, Numerical Aptitude Questions and Match the cases from Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics.
  • The marking scheme will be provided in “Instructions to Candidates” at the time of examination.

JEE Advanced 2021 Previous Year Paper Analysis

On the basis of JEE Advanced question papers of last three years, the following points were concluded :

  • It was observed that the total number of questions per exam has remained the same during the last 3 years.
  • The maximum number of questions had more than one correct option to choose from, making these types of questions the most important ones.
  • It was also found that the questions with more than one correct answer were the most skipped questions during these 3 years.
  • JEE Advanced 2018 saw a change in the question paper with the introduction of Matrix Match type questions and Numerical type questions. In the same year, single option questions and integer type questions were discontinued in all subjects.

JEE Advanced 2021 Mock Tests

Mock tests for JEE Advanced 2021 will be released by the exam conducting body prior to the exam date. The mock tests are designed to give an idea of the actual examination and help the candidates check their preparation level. Mock tests also help the students to manage their time well.

Designed to assist students in identifying their strengths and weaknesses and guide them to prepare accordingly, the mock tests make the candidates familiar with the weightage and the difficulty level of the examination. 

Like the real examination, JEE Advanced mock test will also include both Paper 1 and Paper 2. After logging in to the conducting IITs official website, candidates can simply click on the link for free mock tests and start attempting questions.

JEE Advanced 2021 Counselling

  • JoSSA will conduct the counselling process for JEE Main and JEE Advanced qualified candidates during the month of June 2021.
  • Counselling rounds are conducted 7 times, depending on the availability of seats. 
  • The counselling process involves registration, filling of choices, locking of choices followed by the validation and allotment of seats. 
  • After allotment of seats, students are required to report at the respective college.
